Task 1

Casteljau’s algorithm creates a subset of points n-1 from the original n points, such that each point in the resulting set lies in between the original n points, with t weight in conjunction with the average of its right most neighbor. I implemented it by looping though the array of points, two points at a time (in order). I then computed the new point, added it to a new list, and returned the list.

Task 2

We extend the original algorithm from part 1 by simply adding the z component to the original function in part 1. We then define a function that iterates over points with t and gets the Bezier point from the repeated call to the first function. Our algorithm goes though all the rows, gets the Bezier point with weight u, and collects them. We then use these points, and get the final Bezier point with weight v.

Task 3

I first get the face of that half edge, get the normal, and add it to a list. Then I get the twin half edge, and iterate once around the triangle. This guarantees that I am on the edge of a triangle in the next face, adjacent to the next face surrounding the vertex. I end once I am back at the original half edge. I then add the normals together and normalize the final vector.
