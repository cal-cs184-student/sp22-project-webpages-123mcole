With the first image being with [alpha = 0.05] in the top left and the last image being with [alpha = 0.5] in the bottom right, respectively.

From the images, when the value of alpha is increased, the shadows are less prevalent and the color of the material shows more. In other words, the higher the alpha value, the more prevalent the material color and perceived brightness of it is. It also appears that as the alpha increases, the material becomes less shiny. This is because, as the diffuse lighting approaches the specular lighting, they appear closer and so it appears that it is less shiny and more naturally a brighter color.

Part 4

A pinhole camera model is where the light is restricted to coming through a hole, and the image is the light captured from the rays that make it through the hole. With the thin lens camera model, the light that makes it to the image has to come through the lens, but due to the lens's optical properties, we have more control over how and what light is sent though. This in turn can control how the background objects look, the field of view, and so on.
