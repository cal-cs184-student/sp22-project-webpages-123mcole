Low ks

When increasing the value of ks, the cloth becomes much more rigid and less flexible. From rest, a low ks will result in a slower change in the cloth’s shape and becomes much more stretched out. A high ks will result in the cloth bouncing quickly but not changing much.

Low density

High density

A lower density results in the cloth seeming much more rigid, as now it weighs less so the bending effect is going to be less. As the cloth becomes more dense, it begins to weigh itself down, causing it to stretch more.

As for the damping, when the damping is low, the cloth moves quickly to a rest position and flexes more on the way down. When the damping is high, the cloth moves much more slowly to rest position, and does not flex internally as much, but does reach the same rest position.

A shader program is a program that tells the gpu how the incoming light and material colors are going to be shows on the screen to the user. The vertex shader determines how the vertices are placed in the real world. The fragment shader determines the color of the pixel on the object / material.

The Blinn-Phong shading model consisted of the ambient, diffuse, and specular lighting. It adds these three effects together into one lighting scheme.
